# ACL 2024 Best Resource Papers

This repository contains information about the ACL 2024 Best Resource Papers - a collection of outstanding research resources from the 62nd Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics.

## Papers and Repositories

- Latxa: An Open Language Model and Evaluation Suite for Basque -- https://github.com/hitz-zentroa/latxa
- Dolma: an Open Corpus of Three Trillion Tokens for Language Model Pretraining Research -- https://github.com/allenai/dolma
- AppWorld: A Controllable World of Apps and People for Benchmarking Interactive Coding Agents -- https://github.com/StonyBrookNLP/appworld

## About ACL 2024

The 62nd Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (ACL 2024) took place in Bangkok, Thailand from August 11-16, 2024. These papers were recognized as the best resource papers, highlighting significant contributions to the NLP research community in terms of datasets, tools, and resources.

## Paper Details

### 1. Latxa: An Open Language Model and Evaluation Suite for Basque
- **Authors**: Julen Etxaniz, Oscar Sainz, Naiara Perez Miguel, Itziar Aldabe, German Rigau, Eneko Agirre, Aitor Ormazabal, Mikel Artetxe, Aitor Soroa
- **Repository**: https://github.com/hitz-zentroa/latxa
- **Description**: An open language model and comprehensive evaluation suite specifically designed for the Basque language.

### 2. Dolma: an Open Corpus of Three Trillion Tokens for Language Model Pretraining Research
- **Authors**: Luca Soldaini, Rodney Kinney, Akshita Bhagia, Dustin Schwenk, David Atkinson, Russell Authur, Ben Bogin, Khyathi Chandu, Jennifer Dumas, Yanai Elazar, Valentin Hofmann, Ananya Harsh Jha, Sachin Kumar, Li Lucy, Xinxi Lyu, Nathan Lambert, Ian Magnusson, Jacob Morrison, Niklas Muennighoff, Aakanksha Naik, Crystal Nam, Matthew E Peters, Abhilasha Ravichander, Kyle Richardson, Zejiang Shen, Emma Strubell, Nishant Subramani, Oyvind Tafjord, Evan Pete Walsh, Luke Zettlemoyer, Noah A. Smith, Hannaneh Hajishirzi, Iz Beltagy, Dirk Groeneveld, Jesse Dodge, Kyle Lo
- **Repository**: https://github.com/allenai/dolma
- **Description**: A massive open corpus containing three trillion tokens for language model pretraining research, providing crucial data resources for the research community.

### 3. AppWorld: A Controllable World of Apps and People for Benchmarking Interactive Coding Agents
- **Authors**: Harsh Trivedi, Tushar Khot, Mareike Hartmann, Ruskin Manku, Vinty Dong, Edward Li, Shashank Gupta, Ashish Sabharwal, Niranjan Balasubramanian
- **Repository**: https://github.com/StonyBrookNLP/appworld
- **Description**: A comprehensive benchmark environment designed for evaluating and testing interactive coding agents in realistic application scenarios.
